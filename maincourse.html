Main Course

A main course, often known as the entr√©e, is the central and substantial part of a meal that follows the appetizer. It is the primary focus of the dining experience and typically consists of a well-balanced combination of proteins, carbohydrates, and vegetables, creating a satisfying and fulfilling dish.

Main courses vary widely across cuisines and cultures, reflecting the diversity of ingredients and culinary traditions. They can include a range of options such as meats, poultry, fish, vegetarian dishes, and more.

The preparation of a main course often involves sophisticated cooking techniques, seasoning, and attention to detail. It is the centerpiece of the meal, showcasing the chef's skills and creativity in crafting a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

Common components of a main course may include a protein source like grilled chicken, seared fish, or a hearty vegetarian option, accompanied by starches such as rice, pasta, or potatoes, and a variety of vegetables or side dishes to enhance the overall dining experience.

Main courses play a crucial role in defining the character of a meal and are often the highlight that lingers in the memory of diners. From comforting classics to innovative culinary creations, the main course is an opportunity for culinary exploration and enjoyment.

Vegetable Biryani

ü•£Ingredients

  • 1 cup Basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 4-5 whole green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

‚åõCooking Time

40 minutes

‚úÖDifficulty

Difficult

üìåInstructions

  1. Wash 1 cup of Basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. While the rice is soaking, chop a mix of vegetables such as carrots, peas, beans, and potatoes. You can customize the vegetable selection based on your preference.
  3. In a large pot, heat 1/4 cup of cooking oil or ghee. Add 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds, 4-5 whole cloves, 4-5 whole green cardamom pods, and 2-3 bay leaves. Saut√© until the spices release their aroma.
  4. Add 1 large thinly sliced onion and saut√© until it becomes golden brown.
  5. Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  7. Now, add the mixed vegetables to the pot and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing them to partially cook.
  8. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% done. Drain the water and set aside.
  9. In the pot with partially cooked vegetables, add 1/2 cup plain yogurt, 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves, 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves, 1 teaspoon biryani masala, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  10. Now, layer the partially cooked rice over the vegetable mixture. Optionally, garnish with fried onions for added flavor.
  11. Seal the pot tightly with a lid or foil. Cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the biryani to cook in its own steam. This is the "dum" cooking process.
  12. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork, ensuring that the rice and vegetables are well-mixed.
  13. Serve hot, garnished with additional fresh coriander leaves. Vegetable Biryani pairs well with raita or a side salad.

Dal Makhani

ü•£Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  • 3 cups water (for soaking)
  • 4 cups water (for cooking)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

‚åõCooking Time

2 hours

‚úÖDifficulty

Moderate

üìåInstructions

  1. Wash 1 cup of whole black urad dal (black gram) and 1/4 cup of rajma (kidney beans) thoroughly. Soak them in 3 cups of water for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, drain the water and rinse the lentils and kidney beans. In a large pot, add 4 cups of fresh water and the soaked lentils and kidney beans. Bring to a boil and then simmer on low heat until they are soft and well-cooked. This may take 1.5 to 2 hours. Optionally, you can use a pressure cooker to expedite the cooking process.
  3. In a separate pan, heat 1/4 cup of butter. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add 1 large finely chopped onion and saut√© until golden brown.
  5. Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add 1/4 cup of cream and the pureed tomatoes. Cook the mixture until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  7. Now, add 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon garam masala, and 1 teaspoon coriander powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the masala is fragrant.
  8. Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the masala. Mix thoroughly and let it simmer on low heat for an additional 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  9. Season with salt according to taste. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a dollop of butter.
  11. Serve hot with naan, rice, or your favorite bread.

Paneer Butter Masala

ü•£Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

‚åõCooking Time

1 hour

‚úÖDifficulty

Moderate

üìåInstructions

  1. Heat a pan or kadai on medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of butter. Once the butter melts, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add 2 large finely chopped onions. Saut√© until the onions become golden brown.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Now, add 3 large tomatoes that are pureed. Cook the mixture until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. While the tomatoes are cooking, blend 1/4 cup of soaked cashew nuts with a little water to form a smooth paste. Add this cashew paste to the masala.
  6. Add spices: 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the masala is well-cooked.
  7. Add the cubed paneer (200 grams) to the masala. Mix gently to coat the paneer with the masala. Cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  8. Stir in 1/4 cup of fresh cream and 1 teaspoon of crushed kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  9. Garnish the Paneer Butter Masala with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Your delicious Paneer Butter Masala is ready to be served. Enjoy it hot with naan, roti, or rice!
